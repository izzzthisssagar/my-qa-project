Boundary Value Analysis

● Core QA ⏱ 6 min read Topic 2 of 4 · Test design
Most bugs don't hide in the middle of a range — they lurk right at the edges. Off-by-one, < vs <=, the "1" and the "100". Learn to hunt the edges and you'll find bugs other testers walk straight past.

Boundary Value Analysis (BVA) is a test-design technique that targets the edges of input ranges, because that's where developers make mistakes.

🏙️
Real-life analogy. Think of a speed limit sign that says 60. The interesting moments aren't driving at 40 — they're at 59, 60, and 61. That's exactly where a ticket is or isn't issued. BVA tests those three numbers instead of every speed on the road.

Key takeaways

  • Bugs cluster at boundaries — test the edge, and one step either side of it.
  • For a range L–U, test: L−1, L, L+1, U−1, U, U+1.
  • Always test invalid boundaries too — that's where missing validation shows.
  • Use BVA together with Equivalence Partitioning for full, efficient coverage.
