📜 The background you asked for — where QA came from

Becomes a full chapter early in QA foundations, written as a story: the 1947 Mark II moth Grace Hopper’s team taped into the logbook (“first actual case of bug being found” — and why the story is half-myth: “bug” predates it, even Edison used it) · Gelperin & Hetzel’s five eras — debugging-oriented (pre-1956) → demonstration (prove it works) → destruction (Myers 1979: a good test finds bugs) → evaluation → prevention (1988→) · the pioneers: Myers, Weinberg, Kaner’s context-driven school, Bach & Bolton’s testing-vs-checking, Crispin & Gregory’s agile testing · then how agile (2001), DevOps, and now AI keep reshaping the tester’s job.
