# 12-Week Content Calendar — QA Mastery

3 posts/week. **Mon = teach, Wed = build-in-public/proof, Fri = engage/CTA.** All organic. Repurpose each piece across LinkedIn + community + (later) blog.

**The 5 content pillars** (rotate them so you're never one-note):
1. **Teach** — actual QA skill (bug reports, test cases, Selenium). Builds authority.
2. **Differentiate** — "you do it, not watch it"; BuggyShop; graded labs. Sells the product.
3. **Build-in-public** — what you shipped, what users asked, the journey. Builds trust + relatability.
4. **Proof** — learner wins, testimonials, before/after. Removes doubt.
5. **Engage** — questions, polls, "what's missing for you?". Feeds the [feedback loop](../02-feedback-loop/feedback-system.md).

## Cadence rules
- **Same days, every week.** The algorithm and your audience both reward predictability.
- **Mon/Wed/Fri**, roughly the same time. Pick a time and keep it.
- **Reply to comments within 2 hours** of posting (biggest free reach lever on LinkedIn).
- **15 min/day** answering questions in communities — separate from posting.

## Repurposing (write once, post 5×)
Each Monday "teach" post →
1. LinkedIn carousel
2. Reddit/MoT helpful answer (no hard sell)
3. dev.to / blog article (Phase 2+, feeds SEO)
4. A design asset (see [design briefs](./design-briefs.md))
5. A snippet for the email course (Phase 3)

## What NOT to post
- Pure "sign up now" ads. (Use the 9:1 value rule.)
- Anything you wouldn't find useful as a tester yourself.
- Engagement bait unrelated to QA.
