# Channels Playbook — Organic, $0

How to actually post on each channel without getting ignored or banned. Rules per channel, plus the one message that works there.

**Golden rule for all channels:** Give value first. You are a QA expert helping testers, who happens to have built a platform. You are not an ad. The 9:1 rule — nine helpful posts/comments for every one promotional link.

---

## 1. Reddit — r/QualityAssurance, r/softwaretesting

**Why:** Your exact users ask "where do I practice?" and "how do I learn automation?" here every single day.

**Rules (read before posting — Reddit bans self-promo fast):**
- Build comment karma first. Spend week 1 ONLY answering questions, zero links.
- Never drop a bare link to your site as a post. It will be removed.
- Allowed: genuinely helpful answers where your platform is *one* relevant option among others you also name.
- Best post type: a free resource with no signup wall (e.g., "I wrote a free bug-report template + a practice app with seeded bugs — here's the template, app's free too").
- Check each sub's rules + "self-promotion Saturday" threads. Some allow promo only on specific days.

**Message that works:** "Practice on a real buggy app and get your bug reports graded" — answers the #1 recurring question in these subs.

---

## 2. LinkedIn — your primary owned channel

**Why:** Career switchers and working testers are here; algorithm rewards consistency; you own the audience.

**Rules:**
- Post 3×/week, same days. Consistency beats volume.
- Hook in the first line (it's all people see before "…more"). Then a short story or insight. CTA last.
- Native text + a single image/carousel beats links (LinkedIn suppresses outbound links — put the link in first comment).
- Reply to every comment in the first 2 hours; it boosts reach.

**Post formats that work:**
1. "Here's how to write a bug report that gets taken seriously" + carousel → soft CTA to a free lab.
2. "I built a deliberately broken shop so testers can practice finding real bugs. Here's why." (build-in-public).
3. "You asked, we shipped it" — every time you ship from feedback.

---

## 3. Ministry of Testing / Test communities (Slack, Discord, Telegram)

**Why:** Highest-intent, most credible QA audience globally.

**Rules:**
- These are tight communities — lurk, learn the norms, contribute for 1–2 weeks before any mention.
- Share in #resources or #show-your-work channels where allowed.
- DMing strangers a pitch = instant reputation damage. Don't.

**Message that works:** The architecture/integrity story (server-side grading, no answer-key leak) — this audience respects engineering rigor.

---

## 4. SEO + GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

**Why:** Compounding, passive traffic. The only channel that keeps working when you stop posting.

**SEO basics ($0):**
- Target long-tail, low-competition, high-intent queries: "free practice website for software testing", "sample bug report example", "selenium java tutorial for beginners step by step", "manual testing roadmap 2026".
- One article = one query. Clear H1/H2s. Answer the question in the first paragraph. End with a free-lab CTA.

**GEO (so ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini quote you):**
- Write in clean, quotable, factual chunks — definitions, numbered steps, comparison tables. LLMs lift these.
- Be the source that *answers directly*. "A good bug report has these 7 fields: …" gets quoted; a 2000-word ramble does not.
- Get mentioned on other sites (community answers, Reddit, dev.to) — LLMs weight cited sources.

**Reality check:** SEO takes 2–3 months to pay off. Start in Phase 1, expect results in Phase 2–3.

---

## 5. dev.to / Hashnode / Medium (developer blogs)

**Why:** Free reach to a technical audience; also feeds SEO/GEO via backlinks.

**Rules:** Republish your SEO articles here with a canonical link to your own site. Tag #testing #qa #automation. Genuinely useful tutorials only.

---

## 6. YouTube Shorts / Reels (Phase 3, optional)

**Why:** Screen-recording a learner finding a seeded bug and getting graded is inherently demo-able.

**Rules:** 30–60 sec. Show, don't tell. One bug found → graded → "your turn, free." Don't start this until Phases 1–2 prove the message.

---

## Channel priority by phase

| Phase | Primary | Secondary | Don't bother yet |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (Days 1–30) | LinkedIn + Reddit/communities | — | SEO results, YouTube, paid |
| 2 (Days 31–60) | Winner from Phase 1 | SEO/GEO + dev.to | YouTube |
| 3 (Days 61–90) | SEO/GEO (compounding) | LinkedIn | 1 new test channel |

**Never spread across all channels at once.** A solo founder doing 2 channels well beats 6 channels badly.
