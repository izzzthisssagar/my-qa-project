# QA Mastery — 30/60/90-Day Marketing Action Plan

**Constraints this plan is built for:** App is **live**, budget is **$0 (organic only)**, founder is **solo, QA-strong, dev-light**, based in **Nepal** targeting a **global** audience.

**The one goal:** Get real testers using the platform, listening to what they say, and shipping what they ask for — so the product becomes what users actually want. Everything below feeds the [feedback loop](../02-feedback-loop/feedback-system.md).

> **North Star metric:** *Weekly Activated Learners* — people who signed up AND completed at least one graded lab. Not signups. Not page views. Activation, because that is the moment the product's value is real.

---

## The funnel we are filling

```
Awareness        →  Click       →  Signup      →  Activation        →  Review/Request  →  We ship it
(community posts,    (link in        (free tier)    (1st graded lab)     (feedback loop)    (RICE backlog)
 LinkedIn, SEO)       bio/comment)
```

Each phase below moves the funnel forward. We do NOT chase vanity reach. We chase *activated learners who give feedback*.

---

## Phase 1 — Days 1–30: "Be present and prove it works"

**Theme:** Show up daily in the exact places testers already are. Get the first 50–100 activated learners. Make the feedback loop real.

### Targets (30 days)
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Activated learners (≥1 graded lab) | 50 |
| Signups | 200 |
| Pieces of feedback collected | 30 |
| LinkedIn posts | 12 (3/week) |
| Community posts (Reddit/MoT/Discord) | 12 |
| First feature shipped from feedback | 1 |

### Weekly rhythm (repeat every week)
- **Mon** — Plan the week from the [content calendar](../03-content/content-calendar.md); pick 3 LinkedIn topics + 3 community posts.
- **Tue–Fri** — Post 1 thing/day. Reply to every comment within 24h. Log every signup source.
- **Daily (15 min)** — Answer 2–3 QA questions in r/QualityAssurance, r/softwaretesting, or Ministry of Testing. Help first, link only when genuinely relevant.
- **Fri** — Run `feedback_triage.py`, review the ranked backlog, pick next week's "1 thing to ship."
- **Sun (30 min)** — Weekly review: fill `targets-and-metrics.md`, note what worked.

### Week-by-week
- **Week 1 — Foundations.** Publish landing page with clear value prop + a 60-sec demo GIF of filing a real bug report on BuggyShop. Set up all 3 feedback channels (in-app form, Tally form, community sheet). Announce "QA Mastery is live" on LinkedIn + your network.
- **Week 2 — The differentiator.** Lead with "You don't watch testing — you do it." Post the BuggyShop story (a practice app with real seeded bugs, graded). This is your hook; most competitors can't say it.
- **Week 3 — Proof.** Share 1 learner's graded bug report (with permission) or your own walkthrough. Ask for feedback explicitly in every post: "What's missing for you?"
- **Week 4 — Ship + tell.** Ship the #1 requested small thing. Post "You asked, we built it." This single post does more for trust than any ad.

### Kill/continue gate (end of Day 30)
If **fewer than 30 activated learners** AND **feedback is lukewarm**, the *angle* is wrong, not the effort. Re-test the positioning before scaling. (This mirrors the Phase 0 gate in your product plan.)

## Phase 2 — Days 31–60: "Compound what works"

**Theme:** Double down on the 1–2 channels that produced activated learners. Start SEO/GEO so you stop depending on daily manual posting. Turn learners into advocates.

### Targets (60 days, cumulative)
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Activated learners | 200 |
| Feedback items | 80 |
| Features shipped from feedback | 4 |
| SEO/GEO articles published | 6 |
| Learner testimonials collected | 10 |

### Plays
1. **Channel focus.** Look at your signup-source log. Whatever channel gave the most *activated* learners gets 70% of your time. Drop the deadweight.
2. **SEO/GEO content engine.** Publish answer-shaped articles testers actually search: "free practice site for manual testing", "how to write a bug report (with example)", "Selenium + Java for beginners step by step". Each ends with a free lab CTA. (GEO = write so AI assistants quote you; see [channels-playbook](./channels-playbook.md).)
3. **Advocacy loop.** Every activated learner gets a 1-line ask: "Mind leaving a 1-line review?" Reviews → social proof on landing page + posts.
4. **Repurpose.** Each SEO article becomes 1 LinkedIn post + 1 carousel + 3 community answers. Write once, post five times.

## Phase 3 — Days 61–90: "Systematize and widen"

**Theme:** Make the machine run with less daily founder effort. Add 1 new channel. Establish the public "you build what users ask" reputation.

### Targets (90 days, cumulative)
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Activated learners | 500 |
| Feedback items | 150 |
| Features shipped from feedback | 8 |
| Public roadmap items completed | 5 |
| Free → paid conversions (first cohort) | track baseline |

### Plays
1. **Automate the busywork.** Use the [automation scripts](../05-automation/automation-overview.md): daily "what to post," weekly feedback triage, monthly review digest. Founder time shifts from doing to deciding.
2. **Public roadmap.** Publish the RICE-ranked backlog publicly (Notion/GitHub). Mark shipped items. This makes "we build what users want" *visible* — a marketing asset on its own.
3. **One new channel.** Pick ONE: YouTube shorts (screen-record a lab), a free email course (5 days to your first graded bug report), or a partnership with a QA community/newsletter. Don't add two.
4. **First monetization test.** With 500 activated learners and shipped requests, gently introduce the paid tier to your warmest segment (Manual Tester Manish — highest willingness to pay).

## What we measure (and what we ignore)

| Track these | Ignore these |
|---|---|
| Activated learners / week | Raw impressions |
| Signup source (which channel) | Follower count |
| Feedback volume + themes | Likes for their own sake |
| Features shipped from feedback | Posting streaks as a goal |
| Activation rate (signup → 1st lab) | "Going viral" |

All numbers live in [`targets-and-metrics.md`](./targets-and-metrics.md). Update weekly. The plan is a living doc — revise targets when reality teaches you something.
